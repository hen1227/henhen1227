Timeline of Projects

This is a record of most of the bigger projects I have done in Highschool.

  • D&D Languages

    23 September, 2021

    After finishing my Morse Decoded App and Braille Decoded, I wanted to continue the idea. This app has been a long multistep process that has gone on for about a month. My understanding of Xcode and my previous knowledge of website coding helped speed up the process, which wasn't that hard. I almost immediately thought of the D&D Languages, Dwarvish, Elvish, and Draconic.

    As of now, I have only completed Draconic. The idea is when someone draws one of the symbols in the app, an AI would determine what character it is. After drawing about 700 images myself, the prototype worked. I used the TensorFlow API. It was more complicated than other methods, but it helped with part 2 of the project.

    What took me the longest was the idea that the users would further train the AI. The app can upload images to be saved on a local database on a webserver from my computer. I made it local because it allowed me to have more control of the images, and with a few simple console commands, I can use the auto-sorted data to create a new model, which can be automatically run every 48 hours or so. This new model is uploaded to Firebase, where it is uploaded and adapted by the app without requiring an actual app update.

  • Fish's Tale

    Mid May, 2020

    My brother and dad liked to fish. I wasn't a big fan, but I occasionally came along with them because I enjoyed keeping track of where they caught the most fish.

    After coding the Copy Paste App, I saw how easy it was to save data on the user's device. I quickly started on an app that would keep track of where someone had caught the most fish. I had hoped to finish before summer, but the project became a larger undertaking than I expected. But I was having so much fun I didn't stop adding feature after feature. In total, it added up to about 18 thousand lines of code. Because I knew almost nothing about the language, I spent most of my time looking stuff up and rewriting the code I just made. I had finished the app around October but kinda lost interest once the fishing season was over. I focused on school.

    Once I got my Apple Distribution license, I published the app on December 21, 2020.
