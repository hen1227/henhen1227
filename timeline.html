Timeline of Projects

This is a record of most of the bigger projects I have done in Highschool.

  • Copy Paste Version 1.0

    Late April, 2020

    This was my first app I have ever coded. I made it on xcode with swift 5. Right after finishing I started work on Fish's Tale. A much more ambitious app.

  • Fish's Tale

    Mid May, 2020

    My brother and dad liked to fish. I wasn't a big fan, but I occasionally came along with them because I enjoyed keeping track of where they caught the most fish.

    After coding the Copy Paste App, I saw how easy it was to save data on the user's device. I quickly started on an app that would keep track of where someone had caught the most fish. I had hoped to finish before summer, but the project became a larger undertaking than I expected. But I was having so much fun I didn't stop adding feature after feature. In total, it added up to about 18 thousand lines of code. Because I knew almost nothing about the language, I spent most of my time looking stuff up and rewriting the code I just made. I had finished the app around October but kinda lost interest once the fishing season was over. I focused on school.

    Once I got my Apple Distribution license, I published the app on December 21.

  • Distribution License

    December 13, 2020

    Once winter break approched and school work lessened I gave my apps another look. After some final touches my apps were ready to be published. My parents invested the 100$ so I can get the Apple Distribution License. After 8 days of realizing how strict apple is about the wording in my app, I published Fish's Tale on December 21, 2020. Then I figured I should publish my Copy Paste Game app. After touching it up, it was published on January 2, 2021.
